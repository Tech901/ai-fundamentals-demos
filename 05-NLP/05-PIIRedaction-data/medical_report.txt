CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Patient presents with persistent belief that he is deceased or does not exist, accompanied by complete absence of pain sensation.

DIAGNOSIS:

Primary Diagnosis: Cotard's Syndrome (Cotard's Delusion)
ICD-10 Code: F22

Cotard's syndrome, also known as walking corpse syndrome, is a rare neuropsychiatric disorder characterized by nihilistic delusions. The patient firmly believes that he is dead, does not exist, or has lost his blood and internal organs. This condition typically presents alongside severe depression and psychotic symptoms.

Secondary Diagnosis: Congenital Analgesia (Congenital Insensitivity to Pain)
ICD-10 Code: G90.8

The patient exhibits congenital analgesia, a rare inherited condition resulting in the inability to perceive physical pain. This condition has been present since birth and poses significant risks for undetected injuries. The patient reports a history of unnoticed fractures and burns during childhood.
